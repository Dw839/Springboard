# Lead Conversion Analytics & Customer Acquisition Case Study

## 📋 Project Overview

This project analyzes customer lead data to understand the factors influencing lead engagement, conversion, and customer acquisition performance.

The objective was to clean, integrate, and analyze multiple datasets to identify trends, measure conversion effectiveness, and uncover potential improvement opportunities in the customer acquisition process.

The project demonstrates an end-to-end analytics workflow, including:

- Data cleaning and validation
- Exploratory data analysis (EDA)
- Data integration across multiple sources
- Outlier detection and correction
- Customer funnel analysis
- Business insight generation

---

## 💼 Business Context

Organizations rely on customer acquisition pipelines to convert potential customers into active users. However, inefficient follow-up processes, poor engagement, and customer barriers can reduce conversion rates.

This analysis evaluates lead behavior throughout different stages of the acquisition journey to identify:

- What drives customer engagement
- Where leads drop off in the process
- Reasons behind unsuccessful conversions
- Opportunities to improve conversion performance

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

The main objectives of this analysis were to:

- Clean and prepare multiple customer lead datasets
- Identify and remove duplicate records and data inconsistencies
- Detect and handle inaccurate values and outliers
- Combine datasets to create a complete lead lifecycle view
- Analyze customer demographics and engagement patterns
- Evaluate lead conversion performance
- Identify major reasons for customer drop-off
- Generate actionable business insights

## 🔎 Data Preparation & Cleaning

Multiple datasets were combined and prepared for analysis, including:

- Lead demographic information
- Demo engagement activity
- Sales interaction history
- Lead assignment information
- Reasons for customer non-conversion

Data quality improvements included:

- Removing duplicate interaction records
- Handling missing values
- Standardizing inconsistent categories
- Correcting unrealistic data points
- Removing invalid outliers

Examples of data issues identified:

- Unrealistic customer ages (100+ years)
- Demo watch percentages exceeding 100%
- Duplicate interaction records
- Inconsistent customer reason categories

---

## 📊 Exploratory Data Analysis

The analysis explored key areas including:

### Customer Demographics

Findings included:

- Majority of leads were between ages **18–24**
- Higher education and job-seeking customers represented a large portion of leads
- Lead sources included social media, SEO, email marketing, and referrals

---

### Customer Engagement Analysis

Analyzed demo video engagement by:

- Language preference
- Watch percentage
- Customer location

Key insight:

- English was the most preferred demo language
- Higher demo engagement indicated stronger customer interest

---

### Lead Funnel Analysis

The customer journey was analyzed through stages:

- Lead Stage
- Awareness Stage
- Consideration Stage
- Conversion Stage

The analysis identified where leads progressed or dropped from the acquisition funnel.

---

## 📈 Key Findings

### Conversion Performance

The analysis identified:

- Total leads analyzed: **354**
- Successfully converted leads: **64**
- Conversion rate: approximately **18%**

---

### Main Reasons for Lead Drop-Off

The primary reasons customers did not convert included:

- Cost concerns / affordability issues
- Preference for alternative learning options
- Lack of time availability
- Lack of interest in the domain

---

### Sales Process Insights

Analysis showed that:

- Early engagement stages require stronger customer nurturing
- Improving demo attendance and follow-up activities could increase conversion opportunities
- Sales teams should focus on leads showing stronger engagement signals
