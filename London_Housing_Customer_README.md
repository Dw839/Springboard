# Customer Acquisition Funnel Analysis Case Study

## 📋 Project Overview

This project explores customer acquisition performance by analyzing lead behavior throughout the sales funnel. Using multiple datasets containing lead demographics, sales interactions, demo engagement data, lead assignment records, and conversion outcomes, the analysis identifies factors influencing customer engagement and conversion success.

The project demonstrates how data analytics can be used to evaluate business processes, uncover conversion barriers, and support data-driven decision-making.

---

## 💼 Business Context

Organizations invest significant resources in generating and nurturing leads, but not all prospects successfully convert into customers. Understanding where leads disengage and why conversions fail is critical for improving sales effectiveness and customer acquisition strategies.

This analysis examines the customer journey from initial lead generation through conversion, helping identify opportunities to improve funnel performance and increase conversion rates.

---

## 🎯 Project Objectives

The primary objectives of this analysis were to:

* Clean and validate multiple business datasets.
* Remove duplicates, inconsistencies, and outliers.
* Integrate lead, engagement, and sales interaction data.
* Analyze customer demographics and engagement behavior.
* Evaluate lead progression through the sales funnel.
* Identify key reasons for lead drop-off.
* Measure conversion performance and customer acquisition effectiveness.
* Develop actionable business recommendations.

## 🔎 Data Preparation & Quality Assessment

The analysis included extensive data cleaning and validation activities:

* Removed duplicate sales interaction records.
* Identified and corrected unrealistic data values.
* Standardized inconsistent categorical values.
* Handled missing data through appropriate imputation methods.
* Merged multiple datasets to create a comprehensive lead lifecycle view.

Examples of data quality issues addressed included:

* Invalid age records exceeding realistic limits.
* Demo video watch percentages greater than 100%.
* Duplicate interaction records.

## 📈 Key Findings

### Customer Profile

* Most leads were between **18 and 24 years old**.
* A significant portion of leads were students or individuals seeking employment opportunities.
* Social media was one of the most effective lead generation channels.

### Customer Engagement

* English was the preferred demo language among most leads.
* Leads who engaged more extensively with demo content showed stronger progression through the sales funnel.
* Demo participation played a significant role in lead nurturing.

### Conversion Performance

* Total leads analyzed: **354**
* Successfully converted leads: **64**
* Overall conversion rate: approximately **18%**

### Lead Drop-Off Analysis

The most common reasons for non-conversion included:

* Affordability concerns
* Preference for offline learning options
* Lack of interest in the subject area
* Time constraints

---

## 💡 Business Recommendations

Based on the analysis, organizations can improve customer acquisition performance by:

* Strengthening engagement during early funnel stages.
* Increasing focus on demo attendance and follow-up activities.
* Addressing affordability concerns through flexible pricing options.
* Prioritizing highly engaged leads for sales outreach.
* Leveraging customer behavior data to improve targeting and conversion strategies.
